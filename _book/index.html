Preface

Photo by Mathieu Stern on Unsplash

When I first stepped into the investment world, there was just too much information all over the newspapers, websites, books, YouTube, and even lunch table gossips which all seemed to be of the utmost importance to me. Someone believed it was perfect time to invest in the stock market, while others insisted that the whole market was significantly overheated by repeated fiscal stimulus packages from the government and we all should prepare for the impending market crash. I was so confused that I soon gave up deciding where to invest my money on my own, and instead relied upon some random 1M-subscriber stock YouTubers, trying to copy what they were doing as closely as I could. It seemed a fairly good strategy until I read what one of the most successful investors on the planet once uttered: "Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing." It was painful because that was exactly what I was doing --- I didn't know what I was doing.

From that moment on, I tried to read what the great investors wrote, collect seemingly helpful information, distinguish between useful and useless ones as best as I could, and make my own investment decision whether it was correct or not. It was not an easy task at first. As I rely heavily on Google to get my work done and get surprised by its versatility almost every day, I thought that investing in Google might be a good idea. I was smart enough to know that Alphabet Inc. was the parent company of Google, so I decided to do some research into it. All I really did was to search it up on Google. In about 0.5 second, Google spat out 166,000,000 results on the screen. It was not long after I scrolled down to see if anything useful was there that I totally gave up. After giving a few more tries to some other companies, I realized that there weren't many websites that fit my needs exactly: something easy, concise, yet informative.

After those struggling days I started to work on this project. It was initially aimed to serve as my personal resource of automatically updated financial data which include only understandable and useful information that can aid me to make unbiased and premeditated investment decision. As the project went along, it slowly evolved into what I thought could also help others who were interested in investing but found existing sources of information exhausting. This project is designed to help specifically those who seek a reliable source of investment information that contain only tidy and unbiased data.

This is a non-profit, public-access, ongoing project still in its infant stage, and I am always open to suggestions from whoever hopes to make it better. Please feel free to contact me, or even better make a pull request (click the edit button on the top left corner of the page you want to edit) anytime anywhere. This project has been hugely inspired by Warren Buffett, D. Town & Town (2018), Graham (2003), and 존리 (2020). All data published on this website are automatically retrieved from various resources and updated every hour. They were last updated at 2021-02-18 10:00:04 GMT. Check them out daily and find out how easily investment can be incorporated into your life.
